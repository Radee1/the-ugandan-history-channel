About Uganda

  • Geographical location

    The republic of Uganda is a landlocked country found in the heart of East Africa. The current president is H.E. Yoweri Kaguta Museveni of the National Resistence Movememt (NRM) political party. Its capital city is Kampala, which is beautifully depicted in the images above. Uganda is neighboured by Kenya to the East, Tanzania to the south, it is south of South Sudan and East of the Democratic Republic of Congo.

  • The People

    According to Historians and Ugandan websites like visit Uganda, the earliest human inhabitants in Uganda were hunter gatherers. Remnants of these people are today found to be among the pygmies in western Uganda. Approximately 1500 to 2000 years ago, Bantu speaking populations from central and western Africa migrated and occupied most of the southern parts of the country. The migrants brought with them agriculture, ironworking skills and new ideas of social and political organization, that by the 15th-16th century, resulted in the development of centralized kingdoms, including the kingdom of Buganda, Bunyoro, kitara and Ankole.

    Today, Uganda is made of diverse cultures and tribes all living together harmoniously. The Ugandan people and East African people in general are known to be very hospitable.

  • Economic Activity

    The major economic activity undertaken in Uganda is Agriculture. This means that for many families in Uganda, the source of income is agriculture. In Uganda, the staple food is matooke( cooking bananas or plantain). Other food crops include cassava (manioc), sweet potatoes, white potatoes, yams, beans, peas, groundnuts (peanuts), cabbage, onions, pumpkins and tomatoes. Some fruits such as oranges, pawpaws (papayas), lemons and pineapples, are also grown.
