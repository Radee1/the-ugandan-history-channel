Uganda National Emblem

Independence Monument

The grey crowned crane, scientifically known as Balearica regulorum gibbericeps, was chosen as Uganda's national emblem because of its beauty and elegance. The crested crane has been Uganda's symbol for nearly 100 years. The crane is definitely an object of great beauty.See About

This monument to Ugandan independence features a man unwrapping and holding a child to the sky. The monument was designed by Gregory Maloba, one of the first graduates of Makerere university's art school. He designed the independence monument with strong elements of ambiguity about both the divided politics of new nation and the dubious prospects for a united future.See History
